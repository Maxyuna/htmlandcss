Ohrid

Ohrid (Macedonian: Охрид [ɔxrit] ⓘ) is a city in North Macedonia and is the seat of the Ohrid Municipality. It is the largest city on Lake Ohrid and the eighth-largest city in the country, with the municipality recording a population of over 42,000 inhabitants as of 2002. Ohrid is known for once having 365 churches,[5] one for each day of the year, and has been referred to as the "Jerusalem of the Balkans".[2][6] The city is rich in picturesque houses and monuments, and tourism is predominant. It is located southwest of Skopje, west of Resen and Bitola. In 1979 and in 1980, respectively, Ohrid and Lake Ohrid were accepted as Cultural and Natural World Heritage Sites by UNESCO. Ohrid is one of only 40 sites that are part of UNESCO's World Heritage that are Cultural as well as Natural sites.[7]


In antiquity the city was known under the ancient Greek name of Λυχνίς (Lychnis) and Λυχνιδός (Lychnidos) and the Latin Lychnidus,[8][9] probably meaning "city of light", literally "a precious stone that emits light",[10] from λύχνος (lychnos), "lamp, portable light".[11] Polybius, writing in the second century BC, refers to the town as Λυχνίδιον - Lichnidion.[12]


In Macedonian and the other South Slavic languages, the name of the city is Ohrid (Охрид). In Albanian, the city is known as Ohër or Ohri and in modern Greek Ochrida (Οχρίδα, Ωχρίδα) and Achrida (Αχρίδα).[citation needed] The name of the city in Aromanian is Ohãrda.[20]
