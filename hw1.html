Skopje

Skopje[1] is the capital and the largest city of North Macedonia[2]. It's the country's political, cultural, economic, and academic centre. Skopje lies in the Skopje Basin. Skopje is in the north of the country, in the centre of the Balkan peninsula[3]. It's neighbouring countries are: Bulgaria[1], Greece[2], Serbia[3], Kosovo[4] and Albania[5]. The river Vardar[4] flows right through the city center, and Vardar being the longest river in North Macedonia. Although Skopje is built on the foot of Mount Vodno, the urban area is mostly flat. Skopje has a humid subtropical cilmate, with a mean annual temperature of 12.6o on average.
